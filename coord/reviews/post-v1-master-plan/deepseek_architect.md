### Stance summary (3-5 sentences)

v1.0.0 shipped with a solid audit foundation and install path, but it carries two structural debts that will bend under their own weight by Week 5: **the entire backup/restore abstraction is still incomplete** (secret redaction + integrity verify are separate rows instead of a unified, versioned `BackupManager`) and **schema versioning is deferred to optional Week 3 polish** despite the fact that the audit JSONL has no version marker and the engine health probe log is an unversioned JSONL too. The most important property to preserve is the **auditability chain**: every data structure that persists operator state must carry a version token, a redaction policy, and a forward-migration hook from day one. I wish v1.0.0 had included a **single, mandatory `SchemaVersion` mixin** applied to `audit.jsonl`, `engine_health_probes.jsonl`, and any future backup archive metadata — because once the ledger grows past 10k rows, retrofitting versioning is a L3 migration pain the solo operator shouldn't have to survive.
